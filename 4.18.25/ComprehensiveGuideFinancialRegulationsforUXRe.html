Introduction

Financial products operate within a complex regulatory environment designed to ensure stability, protect consumers, prevent fraud, and promote fair access. Understanding these regulations is crucial when creating personas, user stories, use cases, and archetypes for financial products.

This guide outlines key federal regulations impacting financial products and provides guidance on incorporating compliance considerations into user experience research. While state regulations also play an important role, this document focuses on federal requirements only.

Major Financial Regulatory Bodies

Federal Banking Regulators

  1. Federal Reserve System (Fed)
  • Primary regulator of bank holding companies and state member banks
  • Sets monetary policy and provides financial services to depository institutions
  • Implements many consumer protection regulations
  • Supervises and regulates significant financial institutions
  1. Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC)
  • Insures deposits in banks and thrifts
  • Serves as primary federal regulator for state-chartered banks that are not members of the Federal Reserve System
  • Manages receiverships of failed banks
  • Monitors financial institution risks
  1. Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC)
  • Charters, regulates, and supervises national banks and federal savings associations
  • Ensures banks operate safely, provide fair access to financial services
  • Issues rules and regulations governing bank investments, lending, and operations
  1. National Credit Union Administration (NCUA)
  • Regulates federal credit unions
  • Insures deposits in federal and most state-chartered credit unions
  • Administers the National Credit Union Share Insurance Fund (NCUSIF)

Consumer Protection Regulators

  1. Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)
  • Regulates consumer financial products and services
  • Enforces federal consumer financial laws
  • Issues regulations, conducts examinations, and handles consumer complaints
  • Focuses on mortgages, credit cards, student loans, and other consumer financial products
  1. Federal Trade Commission (FTC)
  • Protects consumers from unfair or deceptive practices
  • Enforces credit reporting, debt collection, and identity theft regulations
  • Has jurisdiction over non-bank financial companies

Securities and Investment Regulators

  1. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)
  • Regulates securities markets, brokers, dealers, investment advisers, and mutual funds
  • Requires disclosure of financial information
  • Protects investors from market manipulation and fraud
  • Oversees securities exchanges
  1. Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA)
  • Self-regulatory organization (not a federal agency)
  • Oversees broker-dealers and their registered representatives
  • Writes and enforces rules for member firms
  • Administers qualification exams for industry professionals
  1. Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC)
  • Regulates commodity futures and options markets
  • Protects market participants from fraud, manipulation, and abusive practices
  • Promotes open, competitive, and financially sound markets

Other Key Regulatory Bodies

  1. Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN)
  • Bureau of the Department of the Treasury
  • Administers anti-money laundering regulations
  • Collects and analyzes information about financial transactions
  1. Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC)
  • Administers and enforces economic sanctions programs
  • Publishes lists of sanctioned countries, individuals, and entities
  1. Federal Housing Finance Agency (FHFA)
  • Regulates Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac, and the Federal Home Loan Bank System
  • Works to ensure stability and liquidity in the mortgage market

Core Federal Banking Regulations

Federal Deposit Insurance Act (FDIA)

  • Purpose: Establishes the FDIC and deposit insurance system
  • Key Requirements:
  • Insures deposits up to $250,000
  • Grants examination authority
  • Provides framework for resolving failed banks
  • UX Implications:
  • Need to disclose FDIC insurance coverage in account opening
  • Impacts trust-building elements in banking interfaces
  • Affects how joint account ownership is presented

Bank Holding Company Act

  • Purpose: Regulates companies that own or control banks
  • Key Requirements:
  • Limits activities of bank holding companies
  • Restricts interstate banking
  • Requires approval for acquisitions
  • UX Implications:
  • Impacts how affiliated services can be presented
  • Affects cross-selling opportunities in digital interfaces
  • Influences account relationship displays

Federal Credit Union Act

  • Purpose: Establishes federal credit union system and NCUA
  • Key Requirements:
  • Defines field of membership requirements
  • Sets capital standards for credit unions
  • Establishes share insurance fund
  • UX Implications:
  • Membership eligibility validation in onboarding flows
  • Disclosure of NCUSIF insurance
  • Presenting cooperative ownership structure

International Banking Act

  • Purpose: Regulates foreign banks operating in the US
  • Key Requirements:
  • Subjects foreign banks to US banking regulations
  • Requires federal approval for foreign bank branches
  • UX Implications:
  • International customer identification processes
  • Currency conversion features
  • Cross-border transaction notifications

Consumer Protection Regulations

Truth in Lending Act (TILA) - Regulation Z

  • Purpose: Promotes informed use of consumer credit
  • Key Requirements:
  • Standardized disclosure of credit terms
  • Right of rescission for certain transactions
  • Billing error resolution procedures
  • Ability to pay requirements for credit cards
  • UX Implications:
  • Credit application flows
  • APR and fee disclosures
  • Credit card statement designs
  • Payment allocation explanations

Truth in Savings Act (TISA) - Regulation DD

  • Purpose: Ensures accurate disclosure of deposit account terms
  • Key Requirements:
  • Uniform disclosure of fees, interest rates, and terms
  • Standardized method for calculating interest
  • Advertising restrictions
  • UX Implications:
  • Account opening disclosures
  • Interest calculation explanations
  • Fee schedule presentations
  • Promotional rate disclosures

Electronic Fund Transfer Act (EFTA) - Regulation E

  • Purpose: Establishes rights and responsibilities for electronic transfers
  • Key Requirements:
  • Disclosure requirements for electronic services
  • Error resolution procedures
  • Limits consumer liability for unauthorized transfers
  • Receipt requirements for ATMs and point-of-sale terminals
  • UX Implications:
  • Unauthorized transaction reporting workflows
  • Transfer confirmation designs
  • Electronic statement opt-in processes
  • Error reporting mechanisms

Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA) - Regulation B

  • Purpose: Prohibits discrimination in credit transactions
  • Key Requirements:
  • Prohibits discrimination based on race, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age, receipt of public assistance
  • Requires notification of credit decisions
  • Regulates collection of personal information
  • UX Implications:
  • Loan application design
  • Adverse action notifications
  • Optional demographic information collection
  • Credit decision explanations

Fair Credit Billing Act (FCBA)

  • Purpose: Protects consumers from unfair billing practices
  • Key Requirements:
  • Procedures for resolving billing errors
  • Regulates prompt credit of payments and refunds
  • Prohibits certain practices in credit card billing
  • UX Implications:
  • Dispute filing workflows
  • Payment crediting acknowledgments
  • Statement correction notices
  • Return policy disclosures for credit card purchases

Unfair, Deceptive, or Abusive Acts or Practices (UDAAP)

  • Purpose: Prohibits practices that harm consumers
  • Key Requirements:
  • Prevents misrepresentation or practices likely to mislead
  • Prohibits acts that take unreasonable advantage of consumers
  • Bans practices that interfere with consumer understanding
  • UX Implications:
  • Clear and accurate presentation of terms
  • Avoiding dark patterns in financial interfaces
  • Fair representation of product benefits and limitations
  • Transparent fee structures

Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA)

  • Purpose: Provides financial protections for military personnel
  • Key Requirements:
  • Interest rate caps during active duty
  • Protection from default judgments
  • Stay of proceedings while on active duty
  • UX Implications:
  • Military status verification in onboarding
  • Service member benefit explanations
  • Special account handling notification
  • Deployment status updates

Military Lending Act (MLA)

  • Purpose: Provides additional protections for active duty service members
  • Key Requirements:
  • 36% Military Annual Percentage Rate (MAPR) cap
  • Prohibits mandatory arbitration
  • Additional disclosures
  • UX Implications:
  • Military borrower identification
  • Special disclosure presentations
  • MAPR calculation explanations
  • Service member-specific product options

Incorporating Compliance into UX Research

Compliance-Informed Persona Development

When creating personas for financial products, incorporate regulatory awareness:

  1. Compliance Knowledge Level
  • Understanding of financial regulations
  • Comfort with disclosures and fine print
  • Previous experience with regulated products
  1. Regulatory Risk Factors
  • Special status under regulations (military, elderly, etc.)
  • Vulnerability to specific practices
  • Rights awareness level
  1. Disclosure Comprehension
  • Reading level and financial literacy
  • Preferred disclosure formats
  • Attention span for regulatory information
  1. Documentation Comfort
  • Willingness to provide required documentation
  • Concerns about information sharing
  • Preference for paper vs. digital documentation

  • Electronic consent requirements under E-SIGN

  • How do young adults interpret regulatory disclosures?
  • What compliance friction points cause abandonment?

Person with Disabilities

Compliance-Informed User Stories

Effective user stories for financial products should incorporate regulatory requirements:

Banking User Stories

  1. Account Opening
  • "As a new customer, I want to electronically consent to account disclosures so that I can complete my account opening process quickly while understanding my rights under Regulation DD."
  1. Transaction Monitoring
  • "As a security-conscious customer, I want to set up custom alerts for unusual account activity so that I can detect potential fraud quickly in accordance with Regulation E protections."
  1. Dispute Resolution
  • "As a credit card holder who doesn't recognize a charge, I want to initiate a billing error dispute so that I can exercise my rights under the Fair Credit Billing Act."
  1. Privacy Controls
  • "As a privacy-focused customer, I want to manage my information sharing preferences so that I can control my data according to my GLBA opt-out rights."

Lending User Stories

  1. Mortgage Application
  • "As a first-time homebuyer, I want to receive and understand my Loan Estimate so that I can make an informed decision as required by TRID regulations."
  1. Credit Application
  • "As a loan applicant, I want to understand why my application was denied so that I can improve my chances in the future, as protected by ECOA and FCRA."
  1. Loan Management
  • "As a military service member being deployed, I want to request SCRA interest rate reductions so that I can benefit from my legal protections during active duty."
  1. Credit Reporting
  • "As a consumer concerned about my credit report, I want to dispute inaccurate information so that I can exercise my rights under the FCRA."

Investment User Stories

  1. Account Opening
  • "As a new investor, I want to complete a risk profile assessment so that I receive suitable investment recommendations in accordance with Regulation Best Interest."
  1. Investment Selection
  • "As an ESG-focused investor, I want to understand the methodology behind ESG ratings so that I can make informed decisions based on accurate disclosures under SEC rules."
  1. Performance Reporting
  • "As a retirement investor, I want to see standardized performance information so that I can evaluate my investments according to SEC disclosure requirements."
  1. Proxy Voting
  • "As a shareholder, I want to receive and vote on proxy materials so that I can exercise my rights under SEC proxy rules."

Payment User Stories

  1. Funds Availability
  • "As a depositor, I want to know when my deposited funds will be available so that I can manage my finances according to Regulation CC requirements."
  1. International Transfers
  • "As someone sending money internationally, I want to understand all fees and exchange rates before confirming so that I can make informed decisions as required by the Remittance Transfer Rule."
  1. Authorization Management
  • "As an account holder, I want to view and manage my recurring payment authorizations so that I can control my account under NACHA rules."
  1. Error Resolution
  • "As a customer who sent a payment to the wrong account, I want to understand the error resolution process so that I can attempt recovery under Regulation E provisions."

Accessibility-Focused User Stories

  1. Screen Reader Compatibility
  • "As a blind customer, I want to navigate the entire application using my screen reader so that I can independently manage my finances in accordance with ADA requirements."
  1. Alternative Communications
  • "As a deaf customer, I want to request customer service through text-based channels so that I can resolve issues without barriers as required by ADA."
  1. Cognitive Accessibility
  • "As a customer with cognitive disabilities, I want clear, simple explanations of financial terms so that I can understand my rights and obligations as protected by ADA."
