Use Cases for Pre-Prompt Primer Knowledge Bases in LLM Sessions

Pre-loading specialized knowledge bases into LLM sessions creates powerful, context-specific assistants that can provide expert guidance throughout the design and research process. Here are practical use cases for each knowledge base:

Dark Patterns Evaluation

  1. Design Review Checkpoints - At key milestones (wireframes, prototypes, pre-launch), quickly scan for manipulative patterns that may have unintentionally crept into designs.
  2. Competitor Analysis - Upload screenshots of competitor interfaces to identify dark patterns they employ and develop more ethical alternatives for your own product.
  3. Regulatory Compliance Verification - Before launching in new regions, check interfaces against region-specific regulations (GDPR, CCPA, etc.) to identify potential violations.
  4. Conversion Optimization Ethical Guardrails - When tasked with improving conversion, use the knowledge base to distinguish between ethical nudges and manipulative patterns.
  5. Client Education - When clients request potentially manipulative designs, demonstrate the ethical and business risks using the knowledge base's frameworks.

Usability Heuristics Evaluation

  1. Rapid Interface Audits - Upload screenshots during design development for quick heuristic analysis without scheduling formal testing.
  2. Design System Component Verification - Evaluate individual UI components against heuristic principles before adding them to your design system.
  3. Prioritization of Usability Issues - Use severity ratings to prioritize which usability issues to address first when resources are limited.
  4. Cross-Platform Consistency Checks - Compare interfaces across web, mobile, and desktop to ensure consistent application of usability principles.
  5. Design Rationale Documentation - Generate evidence-based justifications for design decisions to share with stakeholders.

The Disney Method Application

  1. Breaking Creative Blocks - When stuck on a research approach, use the Dreamer phase prompts to generate unconventional methodologies.
  2. Research Plan Validation - After creating a research plan, run it through the Critic phase to identify potential weaknesses before implementation.
  3. Solo Practitioner Methodological Support - Individual researchers can use the knowledge base to simulate different thinking modes they might struggle to achieve alone.
  4. Workshop Facilitation Prep - Before leading a research workshop, generate phase-specific activities and prompts for team exercises.
  5. Research Method Adaptation - When adapting established methodologies to new contexts, use the Disney Method to reimagine, implement, and critique the adaptation.

User Interview Techniques

  1. Discussion Guide Development - Generate effective questions and prompts that avoid biasing participants.
  2. Interview Preparation - Before important user sessions, review best practices and techniques to improve information gathering.
  3. Response Analysis Planning - Develop frameworks for analyzing participant responses before conducting interviews.
  4. Challenging Participant Scenarios - Get advice on handling difficult interview situations (dominating participants, off-topic discussions, etc.).
  5. Remote Interview Optimization - Adapt interview techniques for remote/digital contexts while maintaining rapport and data quality.

Research Ethics & Governance

  1. Research Protocol Review - Before submitting for formal approval, check research designs for ethical considerations and potential issues.
  2. Consent Form Development - Generate appropriate informed consent language for different research contexts and participant groups.
  3. Vulnerable Population Considerations - When researching with sensitive user groups, identify special ethical safeguards needed.
  4. Data Privacy Planning - Develop protocols for responsibly collecting, storing, and using participant data.
  5. Incentive Structure Design - Create appropriate compensation models that avoid undue influence or coercion.

Cross-Knowledge Base Applications

  1. Comprehensive Design Evaluation - Sequentially run designs through multiple knowledge bases (usability heuristics → dark patterns → research ethics) for holistic assessment.
  2. Methodology Integration - Combine approaches (e.g., Disney Method to conceptualize research questions + Interview Techniques to implement them).
  3. Learning and Professional Development - Use knowledge bases as educational tools to develop expertise in unfamiliar methodologies.
  4. Team Alignment - Establish shared frameworks and vocabularies by referencing the same knowledge bases during collaborative work.
  5. Client Communication - Translate complex research and design concepts into accessible explanations backed by established methodologies.

These pre-prompt knowledge bases effectively turn generalist LLMs into specialized research and design consultants, providing expert guidance throughout the product development lifecycle while reducing the time and expertise barriers to implementing best practices.
