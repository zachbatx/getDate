Why Exporation Template

Context: Organizations struggle to consistently apply specialized knowledge across teams. While UX guidelines, accessibility standards, and design patterns exist, they often remain in siloed documentation that requires context-switching to access. Current LLM interactions lack domain-specific expertise without lengthy manual prompting, creating inefficiencies and inconsistent results. Research shows that 67% of design and development time is spent searching for information rather than applying it.

Hypothesis: We believe that integrating specialized knowledge bases directly into LLM workflows will significantly improve productivity and output quality across UX, development, and testing teams. The LLM Primer bookmarklet will demonstrate that bringing knowledge to users (rather than users to knowledge) increases both adoption and consistent application of best practices. If successful, this approach could be extended to any domain requiring specialized expertise.

Target Segment (Audience): Primary audiences include UX researchers, designers, product owners, QA testers, and accessibility specialists working in digital product development. Secondary audiences include developers, content strategists, and product managers who need occasional access to specialized knowledge.

Targeted Timing: Initial beta release in Q2 2025 with core knowledge bases (dark patterns, usability heuristics, accessibility). Full release planned for Q3 2025 with expanded knowledge domains and enterprise deployment options.

Variable Changes: Knowledge base content will require regular updates as best practices evolve. User adoption patterns may inform which domains receive priority updates. Browser compatibility requirements may change with new releases. Integration capabilities with different LLM platforms may expand over time.

Measurement Plan: Primary KPIs include adoption rate (% of eligible users actively using the tool), frequency of use (weekly active users), and knowledge application (measured through surveys on output quality). Secondary metrics include time saved compared to traditional knowledge retrieval methods. Guardrail metrics will monitor for any decrease in output diversity or creativity. Initial measurement period will be 3 months post-launch with quarterly reviews thereafter.

Go Forward: Long-term strategy involves expanding beyond the bookmarklet to enterprise-integrated solutions with custom knowledge base creation tools. Future hypotheses to explore include: 1) The impact of organization-specific knowledge bases versus industry standards, 2) The effectiveness of different knowledge structures on LLM output quality, and 3) The potential for automated knowledge base updates through pattern recognition in successful outputs.

Impact: This tool can significantly increase access to specialized knowledge within daily workflows across various roles, enhancing efficiency, identifying potential issues early, facilitating ideation, and supporting collaborative solution brainstorming without requiring additional context switching.
